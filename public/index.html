Before You Start

TVAM is a simple inward conversation.
It won’t give you advice or tell you what to do — it helps you understand what’s happening inside you, clearly and calmly.
What TVAM can help with
You can talk about anything that feels heavy, confusing, stuck, or painful — anger, fear, pressure, self-doubt, stress, or,
“Why do I feel like this?”
TVAM helps you slow down, look within, and see things as they are.
What kind of replies you can expect
TVAM listens carefully, asks simple questions, and shows you the patterns, thoughts, and assumptions that may be shaping how you feel.
The tone is warm, direct, and easy to understand — never judging, never giving orders.
Examples of what you can ask
• “Why do I get angry so fast?”
• “Why do I feel stuck?”
• “Why am I scared for no reason?”
• “Why does this hurt so much?”
• “What is happening inside me right now?”
What you should look for
A small shift is enough: a clearer thought, a quieter mind, a softer feeling, or a new insight about yourself.
These small changes show that inward clarity is beginning.
Why this test matters
We’re learning whether simple awareness — without therapy, advice, or techniques — can help people feel calmer, clearer, and more stable in everyday life.
A few tips
• Talk about one feeling or question at a time.
• Keep your messages simple.
• If something feels unclear, say “I don’t understand.”
• You can stop at any time by saying “That’s enough.”
When you’re ready, begin with: “Right now, I feel…”
